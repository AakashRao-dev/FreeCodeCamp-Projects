Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was a visionary warrior-king who founded the Maratha Empire and challenged the mighty Mughal Empire with courage, strategy, and purpose.

Time Line of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

  • 1630 – Born at Shivneri Fort in Maharashtra into the Bhonsle Maratha clan, marking the beginning of a legendary warrior's life.
  • 1645 – At just 15, he took a historic oath with his comrades to establish 'Swarajya'—self-rule free from foreign domination.
  • 1647 – Captured Torna Fort, his first major military achievement, demonstrating early signs of leadership and strategy.
  • 1656 – Successfully seized strategic forts like Chakan and Kondana (later renamed Sinhagad), expanding his territory and influence.
  • 1659 – Ingeniously defeated Afzal Khan of the Bijapur Sultanate in a dramatic encounter at Pratapgad, boosting Maratha morale.
  • 1664 – Conducted a bold raid on the prosperous Mughal city of Surat, asserting Maratha dominance and securing crucial funds.
  • 1666 – Executed a daring escape from house arrest in Agra under Aurangzeb, using strategic planning and clever disguise.
  • 1674 – Formally crowned as Chhatrapati at Raigad Fort, establishing the sovereign Maratha Empire and promoting Hindavi Swarajya.
  • 1680 – Passed away at Raigad Fort, leaving behind a legacy of valor, governance, and the foundations of a strong Maratha Empire.
